RAVA IDLI

Ingredients

Rava suji 1.5 cups
Mustard seeds 1 teaspoon
cumin 1/2 teaspoon
salt 1 teaspoon
Refined oil 1 teaspoon
carrot 1 unit
curry leaves 4g
ginger 5g
french beans 1/2 cups
curd 150g
ghee 1 tablespoon
water 1/2 cups
green chillies 2 units
coriander leaves4g

Did you know that a South Indian breakfast is considered to be one of the healthiest in the whole world? This delicious rawa idli recipe is a part of South Indian cuisine and lends a tasty twist to the classic rice and lentil cakes. Combining the goodness of pulses, granulated wheat, and spices, rawa idli has gained popularity from the tiffin centres of Karnataka as this instant recipe bypasses the task of prior soaking, grinding, and fermentation. Instead of using premixed rawa idlis that contain preservatives, make this rawa idli restaurant style at home to make a healthy snack for your entire family. You can even pack two of these idlis for your child’s snack box. Nothing can beat the delicious taste of a freshly steamed rawa idli accompanied by traditional dips like coconut chutney, sambhar or podi powder. Moreover, you can find all the ingredients used in this dish in your kitchen or nearby neighborhood shop. If you’re on a vegan diet prepare rawa idli at home using non-dairy or low-fat yogurt. Cashew nuts add to the nutritive value of the idlis while grated ginger makes them more flavourful. Sour curd lends softness to the idlis. Use finely chopped carrots, cabbage or onion in the batter for adding variation to this dish. Steaming the idlis on a medium flame makes them more spongy. Dry roast the semolina before using or else the idlis might turn sticky. Baking soda or a pinch of eno salt can hasten the fermentation process of this idli batter.

Recipe by: Naman Jain

Directions

  1. Fine chop the green chilli and ginger. grate the carrot. Set aside Heat ghee in a pan and add mustard, cumin, chana dal. Saute until the dal turns golden. Reduce the flame and add curry leaves, chopped green chilli, chopped ginger. Saute till fragrant. Add rava and mix well. Roast on a low flame mixing continuously until the rava turns aromatic. Set aside.

  2. When the rava cools down completely, add salt, chopped coriander, curd and 1/2 cup water. Mix well until a thick batter is formed. Cover and set aside for 30 minutes. Bring water to a boil in a steamer.

  3. Grease the moulds. Sprinkle some grated carrots and set aside. The batter has to be thick like regular idli batter and must be smooth.

  4. Add fruit salt, chopped green beans, and dill leaves. Gently give it a mix to combine. The batter needs to be steamed immediately otherwise the rava idli will not turn fluffy and soft.

  5. Pour the batter into the greased mould. Steam for 10-12 minutes on a high flame.

  6. Once the idli is cooked allow it to cool down for about 3-4 minutes and demould them.

  7. Serve rava idli with coconut chutney.
